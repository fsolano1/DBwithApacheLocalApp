Binary Classifier Limitations on Multi-Class Data

Why is the alignment accuracy lower than expected?

The dataset loaded from Kaggle containing validation tweets uses 4 distinct labels: Positive, Negative, Neutral, and Irrelevant.

However, the local model deployed (distilbert-base-uncased-finetuned-sst-2-english) is a binary sentiment classifier. It is mathematically constrained to classify any given text as either POSITIVE or NEGATIVE.

Consequently, when the model evaluates a tweet that is labeled as Neutral or Irrelevant in the ground-truth dataset, it is forced to force-map it. This creates automatic "mismatching" status reports in our comparison table, lowering the net validation score.

lightbulb Key Recommendation

To achieve higher alignment accuracy on datasets with neutral content, replace the classifier model in app.py with a multi-class sentiment model, such as cardiffnlp/twitter-roberta-base-sentiment, which supports positive, negative, and neutral classes.
